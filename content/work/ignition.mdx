## Problem

The standard founder journey is a one-person stack of full-time jobs: researcher, designer, brand strategist, engineer, marketer, launcher. The founder is bad at most of them. The result is six months of half-built ideas. Most never make it to first dollar.

## Build

Ignition is a multi-agent swarm that takes the founder out of the wrong jobs. Twelve specialized agents coordinated by a planner: a market researcher, a brand strategist, a naming agent, a copywriter, a designer, an engineer, a launch coordinator. Each one is tuned for its job and hands its output to the next.

Built on Next.js 14 with Prisma for the data layer, Trigger.dev for the orchestration, and a mixed-model setup of Claude and Gemini depending on the task profile. Claude handles reasoning, Gemini handles long-context research. The founder talks to one chat. The chat talks to the swarm.

## Outcome

Founders who used to ship one half-baked idea every six months ship three production-grade prototypes a month. The product quality is higher because the agents are doing what they are good at and the founder is doing what they are good at.

## Stack

Next.js 14, Prisma, Trigger.dev, Anthropic Claude, Google Gemini. Same multi-model architecture we use for any project that needs both deep reasoning and long-context research.

## What this is, generally

A working example of what coordinated AI collaboration looks like at the small-team scale. Not a chat interface. A swarm. The future of solo founding looks more like this and less like one tab of ChatGPT.
