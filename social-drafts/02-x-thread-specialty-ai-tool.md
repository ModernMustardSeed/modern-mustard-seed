# X Thread: The $3K → $99 Specialty AI Tool

**Hook (post 1/9):**
Every industry has a $3K line item that AI now does in seconds.

Real estate: $3K virtual staging.
Law: $5K document review.
Healthcare: $2K intake summaries.

The line item is still on the invoice.

That gap is the opportunity 👇

**2/9:**
The framework. Pick a workflow that:

- Costs $1K-$5K
- Takes 2 days to 2 weeks
- Is mostly cognitive labor AI now handles
- Has clear input → clear output
- Is purchased by a non-technical buyer

3 of 5 = candidate. 4 of 5 = product.

**3/9:**
Real examples I have shipped:

🏠 Luxe Design ($3K staging → $99/mo)
🏘️ DEED AI ($30K agent commission → $99/mo command center)
🏗️ PTG AI Deal Analyzer (2 hours → 90 seconds)

Same pattern, three different industries.

**4/9:**
The validation rule:

Do not build the thing. Build the test first.

1. Find 10 real buyers
2. Mock the output BY HAND with ChatGPT
3. Show 5 of them. Ask: "Would you pay $X/month for unlimited?"
4. Watch their face

The face is the answer.

**5/9:**
The build:

- Single screen (input → output → history)
- Pick the model that FITS THE TASK (not your favorite)
- Bill per-use OR subscription. Not both. Pick one.
- Auth from day one. No "we'll add it later."

3 to 4 weeks from validation to launch is normal.

Past 4 weeks = over-building.

**6/9:**
The pricing trap:

You'll be tempted to price as % of the thing you replaced. "$3K staging, so I'll charge $1K."

Wrong.

Price for the volume your customer will actually do, plus margin.

Most specialty AI tools land $49-$499/mo. Above = competing w/ full service. Below = not a business.

**7/9:**
The launch:

Buyer pool is small and specific. Do NOT buy ads.

- DM the 50 most active buyers in the industry
- One 90-second demo Loom
- 2-week free trial w/ credit card up front
- One reference logo from the first 3 signups

Direct distribution beats broad acquisition every time.

**8/9:**
What kills specialty AI tools:

1. Building for "everyone in the industry" instead of a specific buyer
2. Pricing too low because you feel guilty about AI margin
3. No human-in-the-loop for edge cases
4. Over-engineering before validation
5. Treating it like SaaS economics (it isn't)
