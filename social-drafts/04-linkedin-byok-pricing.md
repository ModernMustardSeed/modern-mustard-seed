# LinkedIn Post: AI Pricing Models

**Headline:**
The single biggest reason early AI products stall is pricing the wrong way.

**Body:**

There are three pricing models for AI products.

1. Flat subscription
2. Metered / pay-as-you-go
3. BYOK (Bring Your Own Key)

Most teams pick subscription because that's what SaaS does.

For AI products, that's often the WRONG call.

Here's why:

In SaaS, the marginal cost of a user is ~zero. In AI, the marginal cost is REAL and scales with engagement.

Your most engaged users are also your most expensive.

Past a threshold, your power users become loss leaders.

The pricing model has to match the unit economics, not the metaphor.

When subscription works:
→ Usage is predictable per user
→ Model cost is small fraction of price
→ Customer values predictability over savings

When metered wins:
→ Usage varies wildly across users
→ Model cost is significant
→ Customer is comfortable with consumption pricing (devs, agencies)
→ You can show ROI per use clearly

When BYOK is the answer:
→ Model cost would dominate the price
→ Customer is technical / AI-fluent
→ Customer wants control over which model
→ You charge for the value layer, not the inference

The combo model (often the right answer):
- FREE or BYOK for power users who want control
- METERED CREDITS for the broad market
- SUBSCRIPTION TIER for high-volume users who want predictability

This sounds complex. It isn't, if Stripe is set up correctly. Same primitive runs all three.

The most common pricing mistake?

Pricing too low.

Every AI product I've built was underpriced on day one. The fix was always painful.

Bias high. You can always cut.
