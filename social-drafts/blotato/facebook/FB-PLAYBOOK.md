# Main Street AI on Facebook: the organic growth playbook

**Owner:** Sarah / Modern Mustard Seed
**Audience:** US small business owners, national. Service businesses first (home services, salons, restaurants, med spas, auto, dental, cleaning, landscaping).
**Goal:** grow a following fast, become the person SMB owners go to for AI help, build a real community.
**Conversion goal:** free forged demo at `/sidekick`. Never a price, never a hard pitch.
**Started:** 2026-07-23. Launch date: Monday 2026-07-27.

---

## 1. Why Facebook is a different build than LinkedIn and X

The Main Street AI engine already runs on LinkedIn (22982) and X (@modmustardseed, 22771), scheduled through 9/20. Do not copy that lane onto Facebook. Three reasons:

1. **Page organic reach is 1-2%.** Posting text to a Page with no followers reaches nobody. On Facebook, the growth surface is **Reels**, which get pushed to people who do not follow you. Everything else is retention, not acquisition.
2. **Facebook punishes outbound links in the post body.** The LI/X posts end with `modernmustardseed.com/sidekick` inline. On Facebook that suppresses distribution. Links go in the **first comment** or on the Page CTA button. This is the single biggest mechanical difference.
3. **Community on Facebook means a Group, not a Page.** A Page follower is an impression. A Group member is a relationship, shows up in their feed as a group they chose, and can be messaged, polled, and served. The Group is the asset.

**The give:ask ratio is different too.** LI/X runs 4-of-5 posts with a CTA because it is demand-gen. Facebook runs roughly **9 give : 1 ask** because it is a community play. Across the whole first cycle (30 text posts + 24 Reels) exactly **three** pieces carry a `/sidekick` ask: text post `fb-20`, Reel `R5`, and Reel `R24`. Everything else ends in a question, a free tactic, or nothing at all. Break this ratio and the group dies.

## 5. ⭐ The signature moment: Teardown Tuesday

This is the engine. Everything else is support.

**The format:** 60 to 90 seconds. A member comments their business in the Tuesday post. You pick one. You pull up their website, their Google Business Profile, and you **call their business on camera**. Then you name the leak and the one fix. Free, no pitch, no follow-up sale.

**Why this compounds harder than anything else you could do:**
- **Infinite supply.** Every small business has leaks. You will never run out.
- **The featured owner shares it.** They post it to their own page and their network. That is free distribution from someone with local trust you could never buy.
- **The comments are qualified leads.** "Do mine next" is a person raising their hand, publicly, saying they know they have a problem. Every one of those is a `/sidekick` demo waiting to happen, and you never had to pitch.
- **It proves expertise instead of claiming it.** Nobody believes "I'm an AI expert." Everybody believes a 60-second video where you call a stranger's business and their phone rings out at 4:45pm.
- **It is the ad that doesn't look like an ad.** The fix you name is, most of the time, exactly what MMS sells. You just gave it away for free and they know it.

**Rules that keep it honest and safe:**
- Only businesses that **volunteered in the comments.** Never a cold business you picked. That is the difference between helpful and humiliating.
- **Never mock.** The tone is "here's what I found, and you're going to love the fix," never "look how bad this is." You are on their side against the problem.
- **Always give the free version of the fix**, even when there's a paid version. Every single time.
- If the call goes badly for them, say so gently and note that this is normal and fixable. Half your audience is in the same boat and needs to hear it's normal.

**Cross-post:** the teardown Reel goes on the Page. The full-length version (3-4 min, more detail) goes in the Group only. That is a reason to join the group.

## 6. The growth tactics that actually move Facebook

These are mechanical, not creative. Most people skip them and wonder why nothing works.

1. **Invite everyone who reacts to your posts to follow the Page.** Click the reaction count, then "Invite." Facebook still lets you do this and it is the most underused free follower source on the platform. 30 seconds per post. Do it on every post that gets more than 5 reactions. This alone can be a few hundred followers in your first month.
2. **Own the first 60 minutes.** Facebook decides how far to push a post largely on early engagement. Post, then stay on your phone for an hour and reply to every comment. **Reply with a question**, so they reply again. Two comments from one person is double the signal.
3. **Reels: hook in 1.5 seconds.** Not 3. The first frame has to make a scrolling thumb stop. Lead with the most surprising fact, not with "Hey guys."
4. **Text on screen, always.** Most Facebook video is watched muted, mid-scroll, one-handed. If your point only exists in the audio, it does not exist.
5. **20 to 40 seconds.** Watch-through rate is the dominant Reels ranking input. A 25-second Reel watched twice beats a 90-second Reel abandoned at 20 seconds. Cut ruthlessly.
6. **Post Reels natively from the phone, not through Blotato.** Trending audio and the in-app Reels editor are only available in the app, and audio selection is a real distribution lever. Schedule the *text* posts through the API. Shoot and post the Reels by hand.
7. **No naked links in the post body.** Link in the first comment. Every time. This one costs people 70% of their reach and they never find out.
8. **Serve in 10 other groups, 10 minutes a day.** This is the entire cold-start plan for weeks 1 through 4. Find groups where SMB owners ask questions. Answer with a genuinely complete answer and **no link and no pitch**. Your profile does the selling. People click the name of whoever helped them.
9. **Go Live once a week, 20 minutes.** Facebook pushes Live to followers harder than anything else. Take questions from the Group. This converts members to buyers faster than any post will.
10. **Repurpose on a 3-week delay.** Every Reel becomes a text post three weeks later. Every text post that outperforms becomes a Reel. Nobody saw it the first time.

### Target groups to serve in (find and join week 1)

Search Facebook for these phrases and join the largest 10 that allow real discussion:
`small business owners` · `entrepreneur support group` · `home service business owners` · `contractor business` · `salon owners` · `restaurant owners` · `HVAC business owners` · `plumbing business owners` · `cleaning business owners` · `med spa owners` · `local business marketing` · `service business growth`

## 8. Measurement (check monthly, not daily)

| Metric | Day 30 | Day 60 | Day 90 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Page followers | 300 | 1,200 | 3,000 |
| Group members | 100 | 400 | 1,000 |
| Emails captured (join question 3) | 60 | 250 | 650 |
| Reels with 10k+ views | 1 | 4 | 10 |
| `/sidekick` demos from FB | 10 | 40 | 100 |

**The only two numbers that matter:** emails captured (the asset you own) and `/sidekick` demos (the revenue). Follower count is a vanity proxy for those two. If followers climb and demos do not, the content is entertaining and not useful, and you fix it by getting more specific about money.

**Kill criteria:** if by day 60 the group is under 150 members and Reels are not clearing 2,000 views, the format is wrong, not the effort. Switch the flagship from Teardown to a weekly Live AMA and re-measure at day 90.

## 9. Guardrails (the same discipline as the LI/X lane)

- **Never type a price.** Prices derive from `sidekickTiers`. Facebook posts carry no prices at all.
- **No fabricated clients, names, or numbers.** Businesses in the content are archetypes ("a roofer in a mountain town"). Stats are category truths. Real client wins only with written permission.
- **No em dashes.** Periods, commas, parentheses.
- **Teardowns are volunteers only.** Never a business that did not raise its hand.
- **No ranking or revenue promises.** "This is what I'd do" not "this will double your business."
- **The group is not a lead list.** Do not DM members a pitch. Rule 4 applies to you first.
